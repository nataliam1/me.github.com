Postulancy Stage for the Sisters of Jesus the Saviour (SJS)

The Postulancy stage is the initial and crucial step in the journey of individuals aspiring to become Sisters of Jesus the Saviour (SJS). This phase serves as a period of discernment and introduction to the unique way of life embraced by the SJS Congregation.

Duration of Postulancy Postulancy typically spans two years, during which candidates undergo a comprehensive formation process. Within this period, there is a special emphasis on practical engagement with the Congregation's mission and apostolate.

Additionally, candidates gain valuable insights into the SJS charism, which centers on perpetual adoration of Jesus in the Blessed Sacrament and compassionate care for the sick and suffering, especially those who are handicapped, abjectly poor, abandoned, and troubled youths in society.


Apostolic Experience: A distinctive feature of the Postulancy stage is the inclusion of nine months of apostolic experience. This practical component offers candidates the opportunity to actively engage with the mission of the SJS Congregation within one of its communities.
During this period, Postulants immerse themselves in the daily life and work of the Sisters, gaining firsthand experience in providing holistic care and support to those in need.
Through their apostolic experience, Postulants witness the tangible impact of the SJS mission and develop a deeper understanding of what it means to serve with compassion.
This hands-on involvement allows them to apply the principles of the Congregation's charism in real-life situations, fostering personal growth and spiritual development. Throughout Postulancy, candidates are guided and mentored by experienced Sisters and formators who help them discern their vocation and prepare for the next stage of religious formation, which is the Novitiate. The Postulancy stage, with its blend of spiritual formation, theoretical learning, and practical engagement, equips individuals with the foundation they need to embrace the calling of becoming full-fledged Sisters of Jesus the Saviour.
