Novitiate Stage: for the Sisters of Jesus the Saviour (SJS)

Upon successful completion of the Postulancy stage and discernment, candidates who are called to become SJS proceed to the Novitiate. The Novitiate is a more structured and intensive phase of formation that prepares Novices for their religious profession.

During the Novitiate, Novices deepen their understanding of the evangelical counsels of poverty, chastity, and obedience, which they will profess as part of their commitment to religious life. They receive spiritual guidance and formation to help them grow in holiness and fully embrace the charism of perpetual adoration and compassionate care.

The Novitiate stage consists of two years: the first year, known as the "Spiritual Year," and the second year, referred to as the "Canonical Year." In the Spiritual Year, Novices focus on deepening their spirituality, developing a profound relationship with God, and internalizing the Congregation's values and mission. This year is dedicated to intense prayer, study, and reflection.

The Canonical Year, which follows the Spiritual Year, involves a more practical approach to religious life. Novices engage in a three-month apostolic work experience, where they apply what they have learned in the Spiritual Year to real-life situations. This hands-on experience allows them to grow in their understanding of compassionate service and strengthens their commitment to caring for the sick, suffering, and less privileged members of society.

Throughout the Novitiate, Novices are immersed in the Congregation's spirituality and actively participate in the apostolate, learning to apply the principles of compassion and care in practical ways.

In summary, the Postulancy and Novitiate stages of formation within the Sisters of Jesus the Saviour Congregation are essential in preparing individuals to live out the charism of perpetual adoration and compassionate service. These stages enable candidates to discern their vocation, grow in their commitment to caring for others, and deepen their spiritual connection with Jesus in the Blessed Sacrament.
